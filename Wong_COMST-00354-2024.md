## SECTION 3: EQUATIONS AND MATHEMATICAL MODELS

### A. Simplified Channel Model

#### Equation 1: Correlated Channel Coefficients (Reference Port Method)
$$h_n = \sqrt{1 - \mu_n^2 x_n + \mu_n x_1} + j\sqrt{1 - \mu_n^2 y_n + \mu_n y_1}, \quad n = 2, \ldots, N$$

**Parameters:**
- $\mu_n$: Correlation parameter (defined below)
- $x_1, \ldots, x_N, y_1, \ldots, y_N$: i.i.d. real Gaussian variables with zero-mean and variance 1/2
- Reference port: 1st port

#### Equation 2: Correlation Parameter Using Bessel Function
$$\mu_n = J_0\left(2\pi \frac{|n-1|}{N-1} W\right)$$

**Where:**
- $J_0(\cdot)$: Zero-order Bessel function of the first kind
- $W$: Normalized antenna aperture length (in wavelengths)
- $N$: Total number of ports

#### Equation 3: Common Correlation Parameter (Reference Port-Free)
$$\mu = \sqrt{2} \left( {}_1F_2\left(\frac{1}{2}; 1, \frac{3}{2}; -\pi^2 W^2\right) - \frac{J_1(2\pi W)}{2\pi W}\right)$$

**Where:**
- ${}_1F_2(\cdot; \cdot; \cdot)$: Generalized hypergeometric function
- $J_1(\cdot)$: First-order Bessel function of the first kind
- Advantage: Links all ports without a reference port

#### Equation 4: Traditional Antenna System (TAS) Channel
$$h_1 = x_1 + jy_1$$

**Comparison:** Equivalent to having one port in FAS, showing how TAS limits channel dimensions

### B. Fully Correlated Channel Model (Jakes' Model)

#### Equation 5: Covariance Matrix Element
$$J_{n,m} = \text{Cov}[h_n, h_m] = J_0\left(2\pi \frac{|n-m|}{N-1} W\right)$$

**Characteristic:** Full correlation structure based on port spacing

#### Equation 6: Channel Vector via Eigenvalue Decomposition
$$\mathbf{h} = \mathbf{Q}\Lambda^{1/2} \mathbf{g}$$

**Where:**
- $\mathbf{J} = \mathbf{Q}\Lambda\mathbf{Q}^H$: Eigenvalue decomposition of covariance matrix
- $\mathbf{g} = [g_1, \ldots, g_N]^T$: CSCG random variables with $g_n \sim \mathcal{CN}(0,1)$

#### Equation 7: Channel Coefficient as Linear Combination
$$h_n = \sum_{m=1}^{N} q_{n,m}\sqrt{\lambda_m} (x_m + jy_m)$$

**Properties:**
- $q_{n,m}$: Elements of eigenvector matrix $\mathbf{Q}$
- $\lambda_m$: Eigenvalues (ordered: $\lambda_1 \geq \cdots \geq \lambda_N$)
- Follows Jakes' assumption: $\mathbf{h} \sim \mathcal{CN}(0, \mathbf{J})$

#### Equation 8: Low-Rank Approximation of Channel Coefficient
$$\hat{h}_n = \sum_{m=1}^{\hat{N}} q_{n,m}\sqrt{\lambda_m} (x_m + jy_m)$$

**Where:** $\hat{N} \ll N$ (uses only dominant eigenvalues)

#### Equation 9: Average Variance of Approximated Coefficient
$$\frac{1}{N}\sum_{n=1}^{N} \text{Cov}\left(\hat{h}_n\right) = \frac{1}{N}\sum_{n=1}^{N}\sum_{m=1}^{\hat{N}} q_{n,m}^2 \lambda_m$$

### C. Channel Model for 2D FAS

#### Equation 10: Mapping 2D Indices to 1D Index
$$k_{(n_1, n_2)} = (n_2 - 1) N_1 + n_1$$

**Application:** Converts 2D array coordinates to single linear index for analysis

#### Equation 11: Covariance Matrix for 2D FAS (Transmitter)
$$\mathbf{J}^{tx} = \begin{bmatrix}
J^{tx}_{1,1} & \cdots & J^{tx}_{1,k_{(\tilde{n}_1,\tilde{n}_2)}} & \cdots & J^{tx}_{1,N^{tx}} \\
\vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\
J^{tx}_{k_{(n_1,n_2)},1} & \cdots & J^{tx}_{k_{(n_1,n_2)},k_{(\tilde{n}_1,\tilde{n}_2)}} & \cdots & J^{tx}_{k_{(n_1,n_2)},N^{tx}} \\
\vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\
J^{tx}_{N^{tx},1} & \cdots & J^{tx}_{N^{tx},k_{(\tilde{n}_1,\tilde{n}_2)}} & \cdots & J^{tx}_{N^{tx},N^{tx}}
\end{bmatrix}$$

#### Equation 12: Spatial Correlation for 2D FAS in 3D Environment
$$J^{tx}_{k_{(n_1,n_2)},k_{(\tilde{n}_1,\tilde{n}_2)}} = j_0\left(2\pi\sqrt{\left(\frac{|n_1-\tilde{n}_1|}{N^{tx}_1-1} W^{tx}_1\right)^2 + \left(\frac{|n_2-\tilde{n}_2|}{N^{tx}_2-1} W^{tx}_2\right)^2}\right)$$

**Where:**
- $j_0(\cdot)$: Zero-order spherical Bessel function or sinc function
- $W^s_i$: Normalized aperture dimensions
- Rich scattering environment assumption

#### Equation 13: Channel Matrix for 2D FAS at Both Ends
$$\mathbf{H} = \mathbf{Q}_{rx}\Lambda_{rx}^{1/2} \mathbf{G} (\Lambda_{tx}^{1/2})^H \mathbf{Q}_{tx}^H$$

**Components:**
- $\mathbf{G} \in \mathbb{C}^{N_{rx} \times N_{tx}}$: CSCG random matrix with i.i.d. entries $\sim \mathcal{CN}(0,1)$
- Both transmitter and receiver have 2D FAS surfaces

### D. Block Spatial Correlation Model

#### Equation 14: Block-Diagonal Approximation of Covariance Matrix
$$\hat{\mathbf{J}} = \begin{bmatrix}
A_1 & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\
0 & A_2 & \cdots & 0 \\
\vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\
0 & 0 & \cdots & A_B
\end{bmatrix}$$

**Where:**
- Each block $A_b$: Constant correlation matrix of size $L_b$
- Correlation parameter: $\mu_b^2$ (often $\mu_b = \mu$ for all blocks)

#### Equation 15: Eigenvalues of Block Correlation Matrix
$$\hat{\lambda}_{n'} = \begin{cases}
(L_b - 1)\mu^2 + 1, & \text{if } n' = 1 \\
1 - \mu^2, & \text{if } n' = 2, \ldots, L_b
\end{cases}$$

#### Equation 16: Block Size Determination
$$L_b = \left\lfloor \frac{\lambda_b - 1}{\mu^2} + 1 \right\rfloor$$

**Where:** $\{\lambda_b\}_{b=1}^B$ are dominant eigenvalues of true correlation matrix

### E. Finite Scattering Channel Model

#### Equation 17: Planar-Wave Geometric Channel Model
$$\mathbf{H} = \sqrt{\frac{K}{K+1}} e^{j\omega} \mathbf{a}_r(\theta_{0,r}, \phi_{0,r}) \mathbf{a}_t(\theta_{0,t}, \phi_{0,t})^H + \sqrt{\frac{1}{L_p(K+1)}} \sum_{l=1}^{L_p} \kappa_l \mathbf{a}_r(\theta_{l,r}, \phi_{l,r}) \mathbf{a}_t(\theta_{l,t}, \phi_{l,t})^H$$

**Components:**
- $K$: Rice factor (LoS strength)
- $\omega$: Phase of LoS component
- $\kappa_l$: Complex channel gain of l-th scattered component
- $L_p$: Total number of NLoS paths
- $\mathbf{a}_r, \mathbf{a}_t$: Receive and transmit steering vectors

#### Equation 18: Receive Steering Vector
$$\mathbf{a}_r(\theta_r, \phi_r) = \left[1, e^{-j\frac{2\pi}{\lambda}\psi(\theta_r,\phi_r)^T \mathbf{n}^{rx}_{1\lambda}}, \ldots, e^{-j\frac{2\pi}{\lambda}\psi(\theta_r,\phi_r)^T \mathbf{n}^{rx}_{N_{rx}\lambda}}\right]^T$$

#### Equation 19: 3D Receiver Port Positions
$$\mathbf{n}^{rx}_n = \left(\frac{n^{rx}_3-1}{N^{rx}_3-1}W^{rx}_3, \frac{n^{rx}_2-1}{N^{rx}_2-1}W^{rx}_2, \frac{n^{rx}_1-1}{N^{rx}_1-1}W^{rx}_1\right)$$

#### Equation 20: Wave Vector Definition
$$\boldsymbol{\psi}(\theta, \phi) = \begin{bmatrix}
\cos\phi\cos\theta \\
\cos\phi\sin\theta \\
\sin\phi
\end{bmatrix}$$

#### Equations 21-22: 2D and 1D FAS Port Positions
**2D case:**
$$\mathbf{n}^{rx}_n = \left(0, \frac{n^{rx}_2-1}{N^{rx}_2-1}W^{rx}_2, \frac{n^{rx}_1-1}{N^{rx}_1-1}W^{rx}_1\right)$$

**1D case:**
$$\mathbf{n}^{rx}_n = \left(0, 0, \frac{n^{rx}_1-1}{N^{rx}_1-1}W^{rx}_1\right)$$

### F. Copula-Based Channel Model

#### Equation 23: N-Dimensional Copula Definition
$$C(b_1, \ldots, b_N; \vartheta_C) = \Pr(B_1 \leq b_1, \ldots, B_N \leq b_N)$$

**Where:**
- $b_n = F_{|h_n|}(r_n)$: Mapped to uniform distribution
- $\vartheta_C$: Copula parameter measuring dependency

#### Equation 24: Sklar's Theorem (Copula Representation)
$$F_{|h_1|,\ldots,|h_N|}(r_1, \ldots, r_N) = C\left(F_{|h_1|}(r_1), \ldots, F_{|h_N|}(r_N)\right)$$

#### Equation 25: Joint PDF via Copula
$$f_{|h_1|,\ldots,|h_N|}(r_1, \ldots, r_N) = \frac{\partial^N C(F_{|h_1|}(r_1), \ldots, F_{|h_N|}(r_N); \vartheta_C)}{\partial F_{|h_1|}(r_1) \cdots \partial F_{|h_N|}(r_N)} \prod_{n=1}^{N} f_{|h_n|}(r_n)$$

### G. Circuit and Antenna Models

#### Equation 26: Equivalent Channel with Multiple Active Ports
$$\bar{\mathbf{H}} = \mathbf{A}_{rx} \mathbf{H} \mathbf{A}_{tx}$$

**Where:**
- $\mathbf{A}_{tx} = [\boldsymbol{\alpha}^{tx}_1, \ldots, \boldsymbol{\alpha}^{tx}_{n^{tx}}]$: Activation port matrix at transmitter
- $\mathbf{A}_{rx} = [\boldsymbol{\alpha}^{rx}_1, \ldots, \boldsymbol{\alpha}^{rx}_{n^{rx}}]^T$: Activation port matrix at receiver
- $\boldsymbol{\alpha}^s_l \in \{\mathbf{e}_1, \ldots, \mathbf{e}_{N^s}\}$: Standard basis vectors

#### Equation 27: Channel with Mutual Coupling Effect
$$\bar{\mathbf{H}}_{mc} = \mathbf{Z}^{rx}_{mc} \bar{\mathbf{H}} \mathbf{Z}^{tx}_{mc}$$

**Where:**
- $\mathbf{Z}^{rx}_{mc}, \mathbf{Z}^{tx}_{mc}$: Mutual coupling matrices

#### Equation 28: Mutual Coupling Matrix Computation
$$\mathbf{Z}^s_{mc} = (\mathbf{Z}^s_A + \mathbf{Z}^s_L) (\mathbf{Z}^s + \mathbf{Z}^s_{LI})^{-1}$$

**Components:**
- $\mathbf{Z}^s_A$: Antenna impedance
- $\mathbf{Z}^s_L$: Load impedance
- $\mathbf{Z}^s$: Mutual impedance matrix

#### Equation 29: Relationship Between Impedance and Scattering Parameters
$$\mathbf{Z}^s_{mc} = Z_0 (\mathbf{I} - \mathbf{S}^s)^{-1} (\mathbf{I} + \mathbf{S}^s)$$

**Where:**
- $Z_0$: Reference impedance
- $\mathbf{S}^s$: Scattering parameter matrix

#### Equation 30: Spatial Correlation for Traditional Antenna Systems
$$J^s_{k(m_1,m_2),k(\tilde{m}_1,\tilde{m}_2)} = j_0\left(2\pi\sqrt{(|m_1-\tilde{m}_1|d^s_1)^2 + (|m_2-\tilde{m}_2|d^s_2)^2}\right)$$

**Where:**
- $d^s_i$: Antenna separation distances (constraint: $d^s_i \geq 0.5\lambda$)
- $m_i \in \{1, \ldots, \lfloor W^s_i / d^s_i \rfloor + 1\}$

## SECTION 4: CHANNEL MODELS

### Overview of Channel Models for FAS

The paper discusses multiple channel models with varying levels of complexity and accuracy:

#### 1. **Simplified Channel Model**
- **Advantage:** Analytically tractable
- **Disadvantage:** May overestimate performance
- **Use Case:** Theoretical analysis and simplified studies

#### 2. **Fully Correlated (Jakes') Channel Model**
- **Advantage:** Accurate, realistic propagation assumptions
- **Disadvantage:** Computationally intractable without approximations
- **Use Case:** Simulation studies, performance validation

#### 3. **Block Spatial Correlation Model** (Novel contribution)
- **Advantage:** Balances accuracy and tractability
- **Disadvantage:** Requires eigenvalue analysis
- **Use Case:** Both analytical and simulation studies
- **Key Benefit:** Tight approximation to Jakes' model with minimal complexity

#### 4. **Finite Scattering Channel Model**
- **Advantage:** Accounts for LoS, NLoS, 3D environments, specific scattering paths
- **Disadvantage:** Difficult analytical characterization
- **Use Case:** Simulation, practical scenarios

#### 5. **Copula-Based Channel Model**
- **Advantage:** Captures non-linear correlations, works with arbitrary fading distributions
- **Disadvantage:** Determining appropriate copula function can be challenging
- **Use Case:** Non-ideal conditions, non-linear dependencies

### Key Factors Influencing System Models

1. **Antenna Architecture:** 1D, 2D, 3D fluid antenna surfaces
2. **Circuit Configuration:** Single vs. multiple active elements, isolation techniques
3. **Spatial Correlation:** Position-dependent correlation structure
4. **Environment Type:** Rich scattering vs. finite scattering, LoS vs. NLoS
5. **Frequency Range:** Determines near-field vs. far-field effects
6. **Modeling Trade-off:** Accuracy vs. analytical tractability

## SECTION 5: CHANNEL ESTIMATION METHODS

### A. Rich Scattering Environment - Deep Learning Approach

#### Unified Asymmetric Masked Autoencoder (UAMA) Architecture

**Motivation:** Exploit strong spatial correlation to estimate full CSI from partial observations

**Mathematical Formulation:**

$$h_U = f_{net}(h_O, \Theta)$$

Where:
- $h_O$: Observable port CSI
- $h_U$: Unknown port CSI
- $\Theta = \{\theta_P, \theta_E, \theta_M, \theta_D, \theta_R\}$: Learnable parameters

**UAMA Composition:**

$$f^{(\Theta)}_{net} = \text{Post-mapper}(\theta_R) \circ \text{Decoder}(\theta_D) \circ \text{Mid-mapper}(\theta_M) \circ \text{Encoder}(\theta_E) \circ \text{Pre-mapper}(\theta_P)$$

**Key Modules:**

1. **Pre-mapper ($\theta_P$):** Non-linear projection, position encoding
   - Multi-layer perceptron (MLP)
   - Non-linear activation (GELU, RELU)
   - Positional encoding techniques: absolute, relative, or learnable

2. **Encoder ($\theta_E$):** Basis vector construction
   - MetaMixer mechanisms with global receptive field
   - Attention mechanisms
   - Spatial MLP (DynaMixer)
   - Dynamic FFT (FNet)

3. **Mid-mapper ($\theta_M$):** Dimensionality reduction of basis vectors

4. **Decoder ($\theta_D$):** Unknown CSI recovery
   - MetaDiffusion architectures with local correlations
   - CNN-like (ResNet)
   - Local Attention (Swin)
   - Graph Neural Networks (GPS)

5. **Post-mapper ($\theta_R$):** Output dimensionality reduction

**Complexity Analysis:**
- Attention mechanism: $O(N_O^2 l_1 + N_O l_1^2)$
- CNN-like mechanism: $O(N c_o^2 l_2^2 + N l_2^2)$
- Overall: $O(N_O^2 l_1 + N_O l_1^2 + N c_o^2 l_2^2 + N l_2^2)$ where $N_O, c_o \ll N$

**Performance Metric:**
$$\text{NMSE}_{h_U,\hat{h}_U} = \frac{\sum_{t=1}^{S_{test}} \|h_U^{(t)} - \hat{h}_U^{(t)}\|^2}{\sum_{t=1}^{S_{test}} \|h_U^{(t)}\|^2}$$

**Objective Function:** Mean Squared Error (MSE)

### B. Finite Scattering Environment - Mathematical Approaches

#### 1. Least Squares Method

**Setup:** Multiuser uplink with sparse channel structure

**Channel Matrix:**
$$\mathbf{H}_u = [\mathbf{h}_{u,1}, \ldots, \mathbf{h}_{u,N}]$$

**Least Squares Estimate:**
$$\hat{\mathbf{H}}_{u,LS} = \mathbf{H}_u + \hat{\Sigma}_u$$

**Drawback:** Requires switching through all N ports, high pilot overhead

#### 2. Low-Sample-Size Sparse Channel Reconstruction (L3SCR) Method

**Principle:** Exploit channel sparsity in finite scattering environments

**Channel Representation:**
$$\mathbf{H}_{o,u} = \sqrt{\frac{MN_O}{L_u}} \sum_{l=1}^{L_u} \kappa_l^u \mathbf{a}_{u,r}(\theta_l^u,r) \mathbf{a}_{u,t}(\theta_l^u,t)^H$$

**Estimation Steps:**

a) **Number of Paths and AoA Estimation:**
   - DFT-based method with angular rotation
   - $$\hat{\mathbf{H}}_{u,LS}^{DFT} = \Omega^H \mathbf{A}_{u,r} \mathbf{K}_u \mathbf{A}_{u,t}^H + \frac{1}{\sqrt{MN_O}} \Omega^H \hat{\Sigma}_{o,u}$$
   - Angular rotation matrix: $\Psi \in \mathbb{C}^{M \times M}$ with rotation parameter $\psi \in [-1/(2M), 1/(2M)]$

b) **AoD and Channel Gains Estimation:**
   - 1-sparse reconstruction via matched filters
   - Dictionary matrix approach

c) **Channel Reconstruction:**
   $$\hat{\mathbf{H}}_{u,L3SCR} = \sqrt{\frac{MN}{\hat{L}_u}} \sum_{l=1}^{\hat{L}_u} \hat{\kappa}_l^u \mathbf{a}_{u,r}(\hat{\theta}_l^u,r) \hat{\mathbf{a}}_{u,t}(\hat{\theta}_l^u,t)^H$$

**Advantages:** Reduced overhead ($N_O \ll N$), low complexity

#### 3. Orthogonal Matching Pursuit (OMP) Method

**Principle:** Iteratively select AoA-AoD pairs through correlation matching

**Process:**
1. Select quantized angle grids
2. Compute sensing matrix
3. Iteratively choose most strongly correlated column
4. Update residual vector
5. Repeat until convergence threshold reached
6. Reconstruct channel (similar to L3SCR final step)

**Advantage:** More accurate AoA-AoD pair estimation than DFT-based method
**Disadvantage:** Higher computational complexity

#### 4. Other Estimation Methods

- **LMMSE-based approach:** Linear minimum mean-squared error estimation
- **Successive Transmitter-Receiver Compressed Sensing (STRCS):** For 2D FAS
- **Successive Bayesian Reconstructor (S-BAR):** Bayesian-based sparse parameter estimation

**Fabrication Methods:**

2. **Microfluidic Systems:** Channels for liquid flow control
3. **EWOD (Electrowetting-on-Dielectric):** Electric field control of liquid position

#### Key Parameters:

**Gallium-based liquid metals:**

**Reconfiguration Capabilities:**
- Frequency reconfiguration: Change antenna dimensions → change resonant frequency
- Pattern reconfiguration: Reshape elements → change radiation pattern

**Advantages:**
- Smooth reconfiguration without discrete switching
- Lower profile compared to mechanical systems

**Disadvantages:**
- Toxicity and environmental concerns (gallium-based)

**Integration Challenges:**
- Substrate compatibility
- Sealing and isolation
- Thermal management
- Reliability in extreme conditions

### C. Pixel-Based Antenna Design

**Principle:** Dense array of individually controlled antenna elements (pixels/ports)

#### Architecture:

**Reconfigurable Pixel Array (RPA):**
- Grid of reconfigurable pixels
- Adjacent pixels can be connected or isolated
- Each connection state can be controlled via switches

**Configuration State Definition:**
$$\mathbf{x} = [x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_{Q_p}]$$

Where $x_q \in \{0,1\}$ indicates whether q-th connection is open (0) or closed (1)

**Antenna Parameters Relation:**
- Radiation pattern: $E(\Omega, \mathbf{x})$
- Input impedance: $z(\mathbf{x})$

**Number of possible states:** $2^{Q_p}$

#### FAS Port Selection:

**Return Loss Matching Constraint:**
$$S_{feed}(\mathbf{x}_n) < -10 \text{ dB}, \quad n = 1, 2, \ldots, N_s$$

**Return Loss Definition:**
$$S_{feed}(\mathbf{x}_n) = 20\log\left|\frac{z(\mathbf{x}_n) - Z_0}{z(\mathbf{x}_n) + Z_0}\right| \text{ dB}$$

Where $Z_0 \approx 377\, \Omega$ (free space impedance)

**Optimization Problem:**
$$\min_{V_l^*} \sum_n \sum_{n'} |||\mu(\mathbf{x}_n, \mathbf{x}_{n'})| - |\mu^*(\mathbf{x}_n, \mathbf{x}_{n'})||$$

**Correlation Between States:**
$$\mu(\mathbf{x}_n, \mathbf{x}_{n'}) = \int_0^{2\pi} \int_0^{\pi} E(\Omega, \mathbf{x}_n) \cdot E(\Omega, \mathbf{x}_{n'}) d\Omega$$

**Optimization Methods:**
- Heuristic techniques (genetic algorithms, simulated annealing)
- Exhaustive search for small codebooks

**Advantages:**
- Multiple active elements possible
- High spatial resolution
- Fast electronic switching
- Large number of configurations

**Disadvantages:**
- Mutual coupling effects (even between inactive pixels)
- Complex circuit design
- Higher component count
- Impedance matching complexity
- Non-linear effects

**Mutual Coupling Mitigation:**
- Isolation techniques between pixels
- Circuit-level impedance compensation
- Adaptive matching networks

### D. Hybrid Antenna Design

**Principle:** Combination of multiple antenna technologies for enhanced flexibility

**Examples:**

1. **Liquid + Pixel Hybrid:**
   - Use liquid materials to adjust pixel conductivity
   - Highly conductive solution for active pixels
   - Low-conductive solution for inactive pixels
   - Address mutual coupling dynamically

2. **Mechanical + Liquid:**
   - Mechanical positioning for coarse adjustment
   - Liquid reconfiguration for fine-tuning
   - Combine speed (electrical) with precision (mechanical)

3. **Multi-Technology Approach:**
   - Different parts using different FAS technologies
   - Optimize each section for specific requirements

**Advantages:**
- Superior flexibility and reconfiguration
- Improved performance through tailored designs
- Address specific application requirements

**Disadvantages:**
- Higher design complexity
- Integration challenges
- Increased manufacturing difficulty
- Potential for unexpected interactions
- Cost implications
